**1.** A bar magnet is placed near and lying along the axis of a solenoid connected to a galvanometer. The pointer of the galvanometer shows no deflection when?
   A) the magnet is moved towards the stationary solenoid
   B) there is no relative motion
   C) the magnet is moved away from the stationary solenoid
   D) the solenoid is moved away from the stationary magnet
   **Answer: B**
   *Explanation: If both the coil and the magnet are stationary, then there is no change in the magnetic flux and hence no current is induced in the coil.*

**2.** From the diagram above, calculate the energy stored in the capacitor
   *(See diagram: https://res.cloudinary.com/aloc-ng/image/upload/v1765611037/ALOC-Questions/Physics/2017/2017_Q_28_lvf0sz.jpg)*
   A) 4.0 × 10⁻²J
   B) 4.0 × 10⁻⁴J
   C) 8.0 × 10⁻⁴J
   D) 8.0 × 10⁻²J

   *Explanation: Given information:

Capacitance (C) = 8µF = 8 × 10⁻⁶ F
Voltage (V) = 10V
Energy (E) = ?

Formula for energy stored in a capacitor:
E = ½CV²
Substituting values:
E = ½ × 8 × 10⁻⁶ × (10)²
E = ½ × 8 × 10⁻⁶ × 100
E = ½ × 800 × 10⁻⁶
E = 400 × 10⁻⁶
E = 4.0 × 10⁻⁴ J*

**7.** An object moves in a circular path of radius 0.5m with a speed of 1ms⁻¹. What is its angular velocity?
   A) 8 rads⁻¹
   B) 4 rads⁻¹
   C) 1 rads⁻¹
   D) 2 rads⁻¹
   **Answer: D**
   *Explanation: Given information:

Radius (r) = 0.5 m
Linear speed (v) = 1 m/s
Angular velocity (ω) = ?

Formula relating linear speed and angular velocity:
v = rω
Rearranging to find ω:
ω = v/r
Substituting values:
ω = 1/0.5
ω = 2 rad/s*
   *(Note: Closest available year from ALOC database: 2018)*
