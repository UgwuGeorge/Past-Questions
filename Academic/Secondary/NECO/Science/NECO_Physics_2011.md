**1.** Body floats in fluid when its …………………
   A) Displaces its own volume of the liquid (fluid)
   B) Displaces its own weight of the fluid
   C) Displace its own density of the fluid
   D) Is no longer subjected to gravity
   **Answer: B**
   *Explanation: Archimedes' Principle states: A body floats when the upward force (buoyant force) equals the weight of the body.
For a body to float:
The buoyant force = Weight of fluid displaced
The key condition:
A body floats when it displaces its own weight of the fluid.*
