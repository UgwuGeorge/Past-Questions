**1.** Given:

Sales ............................₦195,200
Stock 1st January.................₦34,000
Purchases ........................₦126,000
Sales returns ....................₦1,200
Purchases returns ................₦2,000

If the gross profit is ₦66,000, what is the value of stock at 31st December?
   A) ₦30,000
   B) ₦40,000
   C) ₦50000
   D) ₦20,000
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Net sales = ₦195,200 − ₦1,200 = ₦194,000
COGS = Net sales − Gross profit = ₦194,000 − ₦66,000 = ₦128,000

Net purchases = ₦126,000 − ₦2,000 = ₦124,000
COGS = Opening stock + Net purchases − Closing stock
128,000 = 34,000 + 124,000 − Closing stock = 158,000 − Closing stock

Closing stock = 158,000 − 128,000 = ₦30,000*

   D) Right issue
   **Answer: B**

**33.** When a new partner is admitted to a partnership, there is a need to revalue the?
   A) capital of the business
   B) capital and liabilities of the business
   C) assets and capital of the business
   D) assets and liabilities of the business
   **Answer: D**
   *Explanation: When a new partner joins a partnership, the assets and liabilities need to be revalued to show their current market values.
Why revaluation is necessary:

Assets may have increased or decreased in value since they were first recorded
Liabilities may need adjustment to reflect current amounts
The new partner should not gain or lose from unrealized gains/losses that occurred before they joined
Existing partners should get credit for any increase in asset values that happened during their time

What gets revalued:

Assets = property, equipment, inventory, etc. at current market values
Liabilities = loans, creditors at current amounts owed*
