# NECO Commerce Past Questions - 2023

## Questions

**1.** The concept which focuses on consumers in decision-making is
   A) selling concept
   B) marketing concept
   C) product concept
   D) production concept
   **Answer: B**

**2.** A bill of exchange already accepted can be discounted by the holder in
   A) the central bank
   B) at least two banks
   C) his bank
   D) any bank.
   **Answer: D**

**3.** Which of the following is NOT a function of courier companies?
   A) Ensuring international transmission of message
   B) Providing door to door delivery services
   C) Providing efficient means of mail delivery
   D) Undertaking the shipment of export and import
   **Answer: D**

**4.** The Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) is an example of a
   A) cartel
   B) trust
   C) holding company
   D) consortium
   **Answer: A**

**5.** The final link in the chain of distribution is
   A) Middlemen
   B) wholesaling
   C) consumer
   D) retailing
   **Answer: C**

**6.** One of the inputs in production that can be motivated by remuneration is
   A) capital
   B) entrepreneur
   C) labour
   D) land
   **Answer: C**

**7.** Which of the following is the safest means of sending a postal order from Lagos to a friend in Jos?
   A) Special delivery
   B) Recorded delivery
   C) Registered letter
   D) First class mail
   **Answer: B**

**8.** Stimulation of demand is a kind of communication in marketing which connotes
   A) personal selling
   B) sales promotion
   C) advertising
   D) merchandising
   **Answer: C**

**9.** Given
    N
 Sales    15 000
 Opening stock  5 600
 Purchases   9 700
 Closing stock  4 400
 Gross profit  4 500
 Net profit   2 000
From the data above, calculate the rate of turnover
   A) 3.50 times
   B) 3.00 times
   C) 2.00 times
   D) 2.18 times.
   **Answer: D**

**10.** The strategy adopted by a firm that ceases to operate at one or more locations because of inactivity is
   A) rejuvenation
   B) focus
   C) diversification
   D) consolidation
   **Answer: C**

**11.** Loan schemes by thrift societies are
   A) mortgage schemes
   B) conventional schemes
   C) mutual schemes
   D) unconventional schemes
   **Answer: C**

**12.** A step-by-step method of carrying out functions in  an office is known as
   A) procedure
   B) organizing
   C) policy
   D) programme.
   **Answer: A**

**13.** The three components of staffing are
   A) recruitment, selection and placement
   B) recruitment, interview and appointment
   C) recruitment, test and placement
   D) recruitment, procurement and appointment
   **Answer: A**

**14.** Services rendered to the public is provided by
   A) Government
   B) civil servants
   C) professionals
   D) domestic servants
   **Answer: B**

**15.** Which of the following is NOT a means of payment through the post office?
   A) Giro system
   B) Money order
   C) Postage stamp
   D) Standing order
   **Answer: D**

**16.** An organizational structure that is military in nature is the
   A) line structure
   B) staff structure
   C) line and staff structure
   D) functional structure
   **Answer: D**

**17.** Goods that are manufactured or acquired by the seller after the contract of sale are
   A) specialty goods
   B) future goods
   C) specific goods
   D) general goods
   **Answer: C**

**18.** An ancillary to trade which promotes socio-cultural development all over the world is
   A) transportation
   B) communication
   C) tourism.
   D) insurance.
   **Answer: C**

**19.** The central working system of a computer used for data processing is the
   A) memory unit
   B) monitor
   C) floppy disk drive
   D) hard drive
   **Answer: B**

**20.** The difference between indemnity insurance and non-indemnity insurance is that the latter provides
   A) cover for exporters against risks
   B) cover for importers against risks
   C) full payment to the insured
   D) consolation payment to the insured
   **Answer: D**

**21.** A crossed cheque with the inscription ‘not negotiable’ must be paid into the
   A) current account of the drawer
   B) current account of the payee of the drawer
   C) savings account of the payee
   D) fixed account of the payee
   **Answer: B**

**22.** A bill of exchange on maturity is allowed
   A) seven days of grace
   B) five days of grace
   C) four days of grace
   D) three days of grace
   **Answer: A**

**23.** An acquired firm in a merger will always receive a
   A) premium
   B) grant
   C) share
   D) discount
   **Answer: B**

**24.** The major determinant of fire insurance premium is the
   A) type and structure of the property to be insured
   B) usefulness of the property to the owner
   C) owner of the property to be insured
   D) extent of fire damage anticipated.
   **Answer: A**

**25.** Which of the following is not a source of finance to a sole proprietor?
   A) Trade Credit
   B) Overdrafts
   C) Debentures
   D) Leasing of equipment
   **Answer: D**

**26.** One of the obstacles  to achieving the objectives of ECOWAS is
   A) lack of a common currency
   B) colonial linkages
   C) sovereignty of states
   D) language differences.
   **Answer: D**

**27.** Occupations are classified into
   A) production, labour and economics
   B) extraction, construction and manufacturing
   C) industry, commerce and serves
   D) trade, aids to trade and services.
   **Answer: C**

**28.** Gilt-edged securities are issued mainly by
   A) individuals
   B) non-governmental organizations
   C) government
   D) multi-national companies
   **Answer: C**

**29.** The business organization established mainly for the purpose of carrying out specific assignments for a specific duration is a
   A) joint venture
   B) cooperative society
   C) partnership
   D) company.
   **Answer: A**

**30.** The major achievement of ECOWAS is
   A) the expansion of regional trade
   B) the emergence of a monetary union
   C) infrastructure! development in member countries
   D) mobility of labour among member countries.
   **Answer: A**

**31.** The exchange of goods for other goods in home trade is known as
   A) entrepot trade
   B) home trade
   C) barter trade
   D) foreign trade
   **Answer: C**

**32.** When a customer writes a cheque in his own name and withdraws cash with it from his account he is both the
   A) drawee and the payee
   B) drawer and the payer
   C) drawee and the drawer
   D) drawer and the payee

**33.** The production of goods in anticipation of demand is 	possible because of the existence of
   A) Packaging
   B) advertising
   C) warehousing
   D) branding
   **Answer: C**

**34.** A business organization must always consider the overall effect of its actions on the
   A) competitor
   B) product
   C) profit
   D) society
   **Answer: C**

**35.** The members of ECOWAS include
   A) Nigeria, Chad, Gabon and Cape Verde
   B) Burkina Faso, Nigeria, Niger and Mauritania
   C) Guinea, Mali, Cameroon and Nigeria

**36.** An ancillary to trade that easily links suppliers with consumers is
   A) tourism
   B) banking
   C) communication
   D) transportation
   **Answer: C**

**37.** Public relations and advertising are similar in terms of
   A) communicating product information
   B) customer education
   C) the mode of operation
   D) creating a favourable attitude.
   **Answer: A**

**38.** The bulls and bears in the Stock Exchange market help to minimize
   A) the number of shares and bonds sold
   B) price increases of securities
   C) fluctuations in the prices of securities
   D) the elasticity of the prices of securities.
   **Answer: A**

**39.** Which of the following is NOT a role of trade association?
   A) Eliminating competition among members
   B) Ensuring uniformity in mode of operation
   C) Establishing link between government and associations
   D) Promoting members interest
   **Answer: A**

**40.** When a country’s total visible and invisible exports are more than its visible and invisible imports, it has
   A) favourable balance of payments
   B) favourable balance of trade
   C) unfavourable balance of trade
   D) unfavourable balance of payments
   **Answer: A**
