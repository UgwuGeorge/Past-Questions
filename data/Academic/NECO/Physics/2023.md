**5.** What is the relationship mechanical advantage (MA) velocity Ratio (V.R) and efficiency with percentage?
   A) e = M.A/V.R × 100 %
   B) M.A = E.V.R
   C) V.R = 1 ÷ sin θ M.A
   D) M.A = 1/E × 100%
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Efficiency of a machine relates the output to the input of the machine.
Key definitions:

Mechanical Advantage (M.A) = Output force/Input force (what you get out)
Velocity Ratio (V.R) = Input distance/Output distance (what you put in)
Efficiency (E) = (Useful work output)/(Work input)

Relationship:
Efficiency can be expressed as:
Efficiency = (Mechanical Advantage)/(Velocity Ratio)
In percentage form:
Efficiency (%) = (M.A/V.R) × 100%
Why this relationship:

M.A represents the force advantage (output/input)
V.R represents the distance disadvantage (input/output)
Efficiency = (Force advantage)/(Distance disadvantage)
In an ideal machine (100% efficient), M.A = V.R

Answer: A. e = M.A/V.R × 100%
This formula shows that efficiency is the ratio of mechanical advantage to velocity ratio, expressed as a percentage.*

Solution:
Step 1: Understand relative humidity
Relative humidity (R.H) is the ratio of actual vapour pressure to saturated vapour pressure at the same temperature, expressed as a percentage.
R.H = (Actual vapour pressure / S.V.P at air temperature) × 100%

The dew point (7ºC) is the temperature at which the air becomes saturated with water vapour. This means the actual vapour pressure in the air equals the S.V.P at the dew point temperature.
Actual vapour pressure = S.V.P at dew point = 7.3 mmHg
Step 3: Calculate relative humidity
R.H = (Actual vapour pressure / S.V.P at air temperature) × 100%
R.H = (7.3 / 13.5) × 100%
R.H = 0.5407 × 100%
R.H = 54.07%
R.H ≈ 54%*
