# Government (2023)

**1.** The underlying principle of the concept of separation of powers is mainly to
   A) educate people on their rights
   B) ensure constitutional review
   C) prevent arbitrariness
   D) tame the executive
   **Answer: C**

**2.** What is commonly referred to as the association of the government and the governed?
   A) Monarchy
   B) State
   C) Confederation
   D) Federation
   **Answer: B**

**3.** The exercise of power can arise from any of the following sources except
   A) legitimate power
   B) charismatic power
   C) coercive power
   D) arbitrary power
   **Answer: D**

**4.** Which of the following best describes the process by which political values are transmitted from one generation to another?
   A) Political culture
   B) Political socialization
   C) Political development
   D) Political participation
   **Answer: B**

**5.** Maintenance of law and order in a state is the function of the
   A) citizens of the state
   B) executive arm of government
   C) legislative arm of government
   D) judicial service
   **Answer: B**

**6.** A bill that applies to the whole population and is intended to promote the general welfare is known as a/an
   A) private bill
   B) public bill
   C) appropriation bill
   D) money bill
   **Answer: B**

**7.** One of the functions of the judiciary is to
   A) implement laws
   B) represent constituencies
   C) interpret laws
   D) make laws
   **Answer: C**

**8.** The type of government in which political power is exercised by a few privileged people is
   A) democracy
   B) autocracy
   C) oligarchy
   D) monarchy
   **Answer: C**

**9.** A major characteristic of a unitary system of government is
   A) separation of powers
   B) concentration of power at the center
   C) regional autonomy
   D) dual citizenship
   **Answer: B**

**10.** In a federal system, residual powers are vested in the
    A) federal government
    B) state government
    C) local government
    D) concurrent government
    **Answer: B**

**11.** The study of government best serves as a source of
    A) enlightenment to the learner in the political system
    B) skills acquisition for public administration
    C) employment for political scientists
    D) knowledge of political history
    **Answer: A**

**12.** What is commonly referred to as the association of the government and the governed?
    A) Monarchy
    B) State
    C) Confederation
    D) Federation
    **Answer: B**

**13.** The exercise of power can arise from any of the following sources except
    A) legitimate power
    B) charismatic power
    C) expert power
    D) ideological power
    **Answer: D**

**14.** A public declaration of policy and aims especially one issued before an election by a political party or candidate is commonly called
    A) steps to govern
    B) policy paper
    C) guide to government
    D) manifesto
    **Answer: D**

**15.** The media, commonly referred to as the fourth estate of the realm, monitors the activities of government on behalf of the
    A) people
    B) executive
    C) legislature
    D) judiciary
    **Answer: A**

**16.** A political system that allows only one ideology for the state is
    A) socialism
    B) aristocracy
    C) communalism
    D) capitalism
    **Answer: C**

**17.** Public opinion is important in politics because it
    A) influences government policies
    B) protects the government from media scrutiny
    C) ensures accountability of government officials
    D) promotes national unity
    **Answer: A**

**18.** Which of the following is not a function of political parties?
    A) Aggregation of interest
    B) Political education
    C) Political implementation
    D) Mobilization of voters
    **Answer: C**

**19.** In which system of government does the legislature pass a vote of no confidence in the government?
    A) Presidential
    B) Federal
    C) Cabinet
    D) Monarchical
    **Answer: C**

**20.** The determination and execution of government policies are the core functions of the
    A) judiciary
    B) legislature
    C) executive
    D) civil service
    **Answer: C**

**21.** The concept of social justice implies that the state must ensure
    A) sharing of political power with opponents
    B) security of multinational businesses
    C) equality of citizens
    D) definition of foreign nationals
    **Answer: C**

**22.** People exercise political power indirectly in a
    |A) direct democracy
    B) representative government
    C) totalitarian government
    D) monarchical government
    **Answer: B**

**23.** The essence of the concept of devolution is to cater for the interests of the
    A) ruling government
    B) majority
    C) minority
    D) rule of law
    **Answer: C**

**24.** A peculiar problem of the parliamentary system of government is
    |A) abuse of power
    B) frequent change of government
    C) large number of ministers
    D) supremacy of the executive
    **Answer: B**

**25.** The sovereignty of a state can be limited to a great extent by
    A) international laws
    B) system of government
    C) right of a franchise
    D) inalienability
    **Answer: A**

**26.** All laws within a state must be consistent with the
    A) constitution of the state
    B) parliamentary Hansard
    C) political authority
    D) political party
    **Answer: A**

**27.** The political ideology that emphasizes individual liberty and limited government intervention is
    A) socialism
    B) capitalism
    C) liberalism
    D) communism
    **Answer: C**

**28.** The process by which an electoral body reviews and updates the register of voters is known as
    A) voters’ education
    |B) delimitation of constituencies
    C) voter registration
    D) electoral boundary review
    **Answer: C**

**29.** The main function of the judiciary in a democratic state is to
    A) make laws
    B) execute laws
    C) interpret laws
    D) protect human rights
    **Answer: C**

**30.** A system where political power is divided between a central government and several regional governments is called
    A) unitary system
    B) confederate system
    C) federal system
    D) parliamentary system
    **Answer: C**

**31.** Citizenship of a state can be acquired through all the following except
    A) Birth
    B) Naturalization
    |C) Registration
    D) Colonization
    **Answer: A**

**32.** The main reason for the adoption of a federal system in Nigeria is to
    A) promote economic development
    B) ensure unity in diversity
    C) facilitate foreign investment
    D) strengthen the military
    **Answer: B**

**33.** Which of the following is an example of an informal interest group?
    A) Trade union
    B) Professional association
    C) Student union
    D) Ethnic association
    **Answer: C**

**34.** The principle of popular sovereignty implies that political power belongs to the
    A) Military
    B) Politicians
    C) People
    D) Rich
    **Answer: D**

**35.** A major advantage of a multi-party system is that it
    A) promotes national unity
    B) ensures political stability
    C) provides wider choices for voters
    D) reduces electoral violence
    **Answer: B**

**36.** The process of removing a president from office in a presidential system is called
    A) Recall
    B) Veto
    C) Impeachment
    D) Dissolution
    **Answer: B**

**37.** Which of the following is a feature of a state?
    A) Government
    B) Territory
    C) Population
    D) All of the above
    **Answer: B**

**38.** The concept of the rule of law was popularized by
    A) A.V. Dicey
    B) John Locke
    C) Baron de Montesquieu
    D) Thomas Hobbes
    **Answer: C**

**39.** A system of government where the head of state is a hereditary ruler is
    A) Republic
    B) Monarchy
    C) Oligarchy
    D) Aristocracy
    **Answer: B**

**40.** The primary purpose of a pressure group is to
    A) contest elections
    B) capture political power
    C) influence government decisions
    D) provide social services
    **Answer: A**

**41.** Which of the following is a limitation to the exercise of human rights?
    A) State of emergency
    B) Lack of education
    C) Poverty
    D) Political corruption
    **Answer: B**

**42.** The term "gerrymandering" is associated with
    A) voter registration
    B) election monitoring
    C) delimitation of constituencies
    D) counting of votes

**43.** The first indigenous Governor-General of Nigeria was
    A) Sir James Robertson
    B) Dr. Nnamdi Azikiwe
    C) Sir Abubakar Tafawa Balewa

    A) promote world trade
    B) maintain global peace and security
    C) regulate international travel
    D) provide financial aid to poor countries

**45.** A major problem of regional integration in West Africa is
    A) lack of common language
    B) political instability

    A) Western powers
    B) Eastern Bloc
    C) Third World countries

**47.** Which of the following is a function of the Civil Service?
    A) Making laws
    B) Implementing government policies
    C) Adjudicating cases

    **Answer: D**

**48.** The system of Indirect Rule was introduced in Nigeria by
    A) Sir Frederick Lugard
    B) Sir Arthur Richards

    A) managed by the private sector

    A) promote military cooperation
