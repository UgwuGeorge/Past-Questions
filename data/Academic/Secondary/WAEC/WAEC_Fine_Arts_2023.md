# WAEC Fine Arts (General Knowledge in Art) 2023

1. Amongst the answers below, which of them is the MOST correct statement about the study of art?
A) Art is only studied in schools.
B) Art is studied by only talented people.
C) Art can be studied by everyone.
D) Art is studied by only school children.
Answer: C

2. Which of these characteristics is the MOST significant of a resourceful artist?
A) They appreciate their national and cultural heritage.
B) They rely on foreign tools and materials for their work.
C) They understand and apply scientific knowledge.
D) They use tools and materials found in their natural environment.
Answer: D

3. In making an artwork such as painting, drawing or carving, the elements of art are essential. The artist can efficiently work with the elements of art through a careful application of ________.
A) Layouts of Design
B) Principles of Design
C) Techniques of Design
D) Requirements of Design
Answer: B

4. I am a two-dimensional branch of Visual Art that concerns itself with the designing and production of clothes. Who am I?
A) Sculpture
B) Textiles
C) Leatherwork
D) Graphic Design
Answer: B

5. Apart from written records, a people's history is mainly documented through their _______.
A) artifacts and oral traditions
B) clothing and footwear
C) furniture and household items
D) landscape and geography
Answer: A

6. Shading in a drawing is an attempt to enliven a drawn image with the effect of light in view. Which of these essential effects is shading primarily carried out to achieve?
A) Mass and form
B) Texture and color
C) Space and time
D) Rhythm and movement
Answer: A

7. Capital letters are also known as ________.
A) lower case
B) upper case
C) cursive
D) italics
Answer: B

8. Which branch of art is valued for its usefulness rather than its beauty?
A) Fine Art
B) Performing Art
C) Applied Art
D) Abstract Art
Answer: C

9. The creation of depth and distance on a flat surface is known as ________.
A) composition
B) perspective
C) proportion
D) emphasis
Answer: B

10. The principle used when designing artworks to make them look stable is ________.
A) contrast
B) balance
C) variety
D) repetition
Answer: B

11. The primary colors in art are
A) Red, Yellow, Blue
B) Orange, Green, Purple
C) Red, White, Black
D) Yellow, Green, Blue
Answer: A

12. A mixture of two primary colors results in a
A) Tertiary color
B) Neutral color
C) Secondary color
D) Complementary color
Answer: C

13. Chiaroscuro is a technique used to create
A) Texture
B) Strong contrasts between light and dark
C) Linear perspective
D) Symmetrical balance
Answer: B

14. The term 'Still Life' in art refers to
A) Painting of landscapes
B) Drawing of human figures
C) Arranged inanimate objects
D) Abstract designs
Answer: C

15. Which of the following is a dry medium?
A) Oil paint
B) Charcoal
C) Watercolor
D) Ink
Answer: B

16. Sculpture that can be viewed from all sides is called
A) Relief
B) In-the-round
C) Mosaic
D) Collage
Answer: B

17. The brightness or dullness of a color is its
A) Value
B) Hue
C) Intensity (Chroma)
D) Tint
Answer: C

18. A series of points that the eye follows is a
A) Shape
B) Line
C) Form
D) Space
Answer: B

19. In pottery, the process of heating clay to a high temperature is
A) Glazing
B) Firing
C) Wedging
D) Scoring
Answer: B

20. Symmetrical balance is also known as
A) Informal balance
B) Formal balance
C) Radial balance
D) Asymmetrical balance
Answer: B

21. The surface quality of an object (how it feels or looks like it feels) is
A) Value
B) Texture
C) Shape
D) Space
Answer: B

22. 'Batik' is a technique used in
A) Printmaking
B) Textile design (Resist dyeing)

D) Architecture
Answer: B

23. A representation of a person's face is a
A) Landscape
B) Portrait
C) Seascape
D) Mural
Answer: B

24. Which of the following is a principle of design?
A) Color
B) Line

D) Texture
Answer: C

25. The area around and between objects in a composition is
A) Positive space
B) Negative space
C) Perspective
D) Foreground
Answer: B

26. A painting done directly on a wall is a
A) Mosaic
B) Tapestry

D) Frieze

27. The lightness or darkness of a color is its
A) Hue
B) Value

D) Shadow
