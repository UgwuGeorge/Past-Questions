# Chemistry (2023)

**1.** Which of the following represents an endothermic process?
   A) Combustion of methane
   B) Freezing of water
   C) Dissolving ammonium chloride in water
   D) Neutralization of an acid
   **Answer: C**

**2.** An isotope has 15 protons and 16 neutrons. What is its mass number?
   A) 15
   B) 16
   C) 31
   D) 1
   **Answer: C**

**3.** In the periodic table, electronegativity generally increases
   A) down a group and across a period (left to right)
   B) up a group and across a period (left to right)
   C) down a group and across a period (right to left)
   D) up a group and across a period (right to left)
   **Answer: B**

**4.** Which of the following is produced during the Haber process?
   A) Sulfuric acid
   B) Nitric acid
   C) Ammonia
   D) Chlorine
   **Answer: C**

**5.** A saturated hydrocarbon with 5 carbon atoms is called
   A) Pentene
   B) Pentyne
   C) Pentane
   D) Butane
   **Answer: C**

**6.** What type of bond is formed when electrons are shared equally between two non-metal atoms of the same element?
   A) Ionic bond
   B) Polar covalent bond
   C) Non-polar covalent bond
   D) Metallic bond
   **Answer: C**

**7.** Calculate the volume of 0.5 moles of an ideal gas at STP. (Molar volume at STP = 22.4 dm^3)
   A) 11.2 dm^3
   B) 22.4 dm^3
   C) 44.8 dm^3
   D) 5.6 dm^3
   **Answer: A**

**8.** Which of the following is responsible for permanent hardness in water?
   A) Calcium hydrogen carbonate
   B) Magnesium sulfate
   C) Sodium chloride
   D) Potassium nitrate
   **Answer: B**

**9.** What is the oxidation number of sulfur in H2SO4?
   A) +4
   B) +6
   C) -2
   D) 0
   **Answer: B**

**10.** The gas produced when sodium metal reacts with water is
   A) Oxygen
   B) Carbon dioxide
   C) Hydrogen
   D) Nitrogen
   **Answer: C**
