# Economics

**Instructions:** Answer all 50 questions. Each question carries equal marks.

---

**1.** Which of the following is a non-renewable natural resource?
   A) Rubber
   B) Oil palm
   C) Coal
   D) Cocoa
**Answer: C**

**2.** The demand for a factor input as a result of the demand for its output is known as _______.
   A) Market demand
   B) Complementary demand
   C) Competitive demand
   D) Derived demand
**Answer: D**

**3.** If the government stops subsidy on cocoa production, the supply curve of cocoa will _______.
   A) Shift to the right
   B) Shift to the left
   C) Remain unchanged
   D) Become perfectly elastic
**Answer: B**

**4.** A monopolist may enjoy abnormal profit if its _______.
   A) Marginal cost exceeds marginal revenue
   B) Demand curve is perfectly elastic
   C) Expenditure on advertisement increases
   D) Price exceeds average total cost
**Answer: D**

**5.** The supply of light could be met using a candle, lantern, and gas lamp. This is an example of _______.
   A) Complementary supply
   B) Abnormal supply
   C) Joint supply
   D) Composite supply
**Answer: D**

**6.** In a perfectly competitive market, a firm maximizes its profit where:
   A) marginal cost exceeds marginal revenue.
   B) the demand curve is perfectly elastic.
   C) price exceeds average total cost.
   D) marginal cost equals marginal revenue.
**Answer: D**

**7.** If the demand for a commodity remains constant as price increases, the commodity is said to be:
   A) perfectly price inelastic.
   B) unit price elastic.
   C) price elastic.
   D) price inelastic.
**Answer: A**

**8.** Which of the following factors is not a cause of change in demand?
   A) Test on fashion
   B) Income distribution
   C) Price of the commodity
   D) Size of population
**Answer: C**

**9.** The basic economic problem common to all societies is:
   A) Scarcity
   B) Inflation
   C) Unemployment
   D) Overpopulation
**Answer: A**

**10.** Production is said to be complete when:
   A) goods leave the factory.
   B) goods are stored in the warehouse.
   C) the consumer pays for the goods.
   D) goods reach the final consumer.
**Answer: D**

**11.** Assisting members to solve temporary balance of payment problem is the reason for the establishment of the _______.
   A) World Bank
   B) International Monetary Fund (IMF)
   C) African Development Bank
   D) Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS)
**Answer: B**

**12.** A major disadvantage of a sole proprietorship is:
   A) quick decision making.
   B) unlimited liability.
   C) small scale of operation.
   D) privacy of business affairs.
**Answer: B**

**13.** The middleman who buys in bulk from the producer and sells in small quantities to the retailer is the:
   A) Broker
   B) Factor
   C) Wholesaler
   D) Agent
**Answer: C**

**14.** In which of the following economic systems is the price mechanism most dominant?
   A) Mixed economy
   B) Command economy
   C) Capitalist economy
   D) Traditional economy
**Answer: C**

**15.** The marginal product of a factor is the:
   A) total output divided by the number of units of the factor.
   B) change in total output resulting from a unit change in the factor.
   C) sum of all products produced by the factor.
   D) average product multiplied by the number of units.
**Answer: B**

**16.** If the demand for a luxury good increases as income increases, the income elasticity of demand is:
   A) Zero
   B) Negative
   C) Positive
   D) Unitary
**Answer: C**

**17.** Warehousing is an economic activity that falls under _______.
   A) primary production.
   B) secondary production.
   C) tertiary production.
   D) intermediate production.
**Answer: C**

**18.** The study of economics is mainly concerned with how to _______.
   A) rank individuals' wants given abundant resources.
   B) make choices when resources are inadequate.
   C) satisfy every member of all societies.
   D) produce all the goods needed by everyone.
**Answer: B**

**19.** The supply of labor in an economy depends on the:
   A) size of the population.
   B) level of technology.
   C) number of firms.
   D) balance of payments.
**Answer: A**

**20.** Which of the following is considered fixed capital?
   A) Raw materials
   B) Fuel
   C) Wages
   D) Equipment
**Answer: D**

**21.** A debenture holder earns:
   A) Dividends
   B) Interest
   C) Commission
   D) Profits
**Answer: B**

**22.** A small-scale firm located within an industrial estate will enjoy _______.
   A) government protection and funding
   B) technology economies of scale
   C) financial economies of scale
   D) external economies of scale
**Answer: D**

**23.** The most important function of money is as a:
   A) store of value.
   B) medium of exchange.
   C) standard of deferred payment.
   D) unit of account.
**Answer: B**

**24.** Inflation caused by an increase in the cost of production is:
   A) demand-pull inflation.
   B) hyperinflation.
   C) cost-push inflation.
   D) creeping inflation.
**Answer: C**

**25.** When job vacancies are publicized, the government is mainly trying to solve the problem of _______.
   A) residual unemployment
   B) cyclical unemployment
   C) technological unemployment
   D) frictional unemployment
**Answer: D**

**26.** From the frequency distribution table, if the sum of f is 20 and the sum of fx is 64, the mean is:
   A) 2.2
   B) 2.5
   C) 3.2
   D) 4.5

**27.** One of the advantages of a public limited company is:
   A) easy formation.
   B) legal entity.
   C) unlimited liability of shareholders.
   D) small capital base.
**Answer: B**

**28.** The process of increasing the value of a currency in terms of others is:
   A) Devaluation
   B) Depreciation
   C) Revaluation
   D) Appreciation

**29.** Money will serve as a standard of deferred payment if it is _______.
   A) homogeneous
   B) durable
   C) easily recognized
   D) stable in value

**30.** The law of diminishing marginal utility states that as more units of a commodity are consumed:
   A) total utility decreases.
   B) marginal utility increases.
   C) marginal utility decreases.

**31.** The formula for calculating per capita income is:
   A) Total Income / Total Population
   B) Total Population / Total Income
   C) Total Savings / Total Population

   A) Regressive
   B) Proportional

**33.** The main objective of the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) is to:
   A) promote political stability.

**34.** Which of the following is an example of invisible trade?

   A) increasing taxes.

**36.** A deficit budget is most likely to be used to:
   A) curb inflation.
   B) increase government revenue.

**37.** The term 'Halal' in Islamic economics (though relevant to general trade) refers to:
   A) Forbidden

**38.** Which of the following is likely to occur if population grows faster than the gross national product?
   A) Interest rate will decline.

   A) the price of a commodity.
