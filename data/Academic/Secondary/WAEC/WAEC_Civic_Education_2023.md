# Civic Education

**Instructions:** Answer all 50 questions. Each question carries equal marks.

---

**1.** Which of the following is important for sustainable human relations in a society?
   A) Tolerance
   B) Birth registration
   C) Cultural affinity
   D) Rehabilitation
**Answer: A**

**2.** The machinery through which the will of the state is driven is described as:
   A) administration
   B) rule of law
   C) government
   D) custom and tradition
**Answer: C**

**3.** A major attribute an individual will likely exhibit if not contented is:
   A) jealousy
   B) irresponsibility
   C) greediness
   D) laziness
**Answer: C**

**4.** A major achievement that can be attributed to good intercommunal relationship in Nigeria is:
   A) promoting national cultural festivals
   B) curtailing rural-urban migration
   C) promoting peace, mutual understanding and development
   D) encouraging competition among individuals in the society
**Answer: C**

**5.** A person born in Ghana by Nigerian parents and resides in Ghana is a:
   A) Nigerian by registration
   B) Ghana by birth
   C) Ghana by naturalization
   D) Nigerian by birth
**Answer: D**

**6.** God-fatherism in a democratic system of government can lead to:
   A) loss of employment
   B) neglect of followers
   C) economic development
   D) citizen's participation
**Answer: B**

**7.** Road signs are usually:
   A) informative and persuasive
   B) punitive and informative
   C) persuasive and regulatory
   D) regulatory and informative
**Answer: D**

**8.** The quality or habit of appreciating a systematic way of doing things with the aim of achieving set goals is:
   A) Humility
   B) Orderliness
   C) Hard work
   D) Politeness
**Answer: B**

**9.** Execution and implementation of government policies are parts of the functions of the:
   A) public service
   B) civil society
   C) military
   D) legislature
**Answer: A**

**10.** Right to ownership of property falls under the category of:
   A) social right
   B) political right
   C) civil right
   D) economic right
**Answer: D**

**11.** The payment of residential utility bills is a duty of the:
   A) federal government
   B) state government
   C) local government
   D) citizens
**Answer: D**

**12.** Which of the following is a feature of a good constitution?
   A) Independent judiciary
   B) Strong armed forces
   C) Strong intergovernmental relation
   D) A civil and morally responsible one
**Answer: A**

**13.** Inter-communal relationships can be defined as:
   A) process of interaction between different religions
   B) process of interaction between different political parties
   C) process of interaction between different communities
   D) process of interaction between different family members
**Answer: C**

**14.** Which of the following describes the process by which individuals acquire political values and beliefs?
   A) Political socialization
   B) Political culture
   C) Political participation
   D) Political development
**Answer: A**

**15.** The ability of the government to exercise its powers without external control is known as:
   A) power
   B) authority
   C) legitimacy
   D) sovereignty
**Answer: D**

**16.** A system of government in which power is shared between the central government and component units is called:
   A) unitary
   B) federal
   C) confederation
   D) oligarchy
**Answer: B**

**17.** Which of the following is a fundamental human right?
   A) Right to acquire property
   B) Right to form political parties
   C) Right to life
   D) Right to free education
**Answer: C**

**18.** Cultism can best be described as an act of:
   A) forming social groups
   B) joining religious organizations
   C) engaging in secret and illegal activities
   D) participating in community development
**Answer: C**

**19.** The main purpose of the rule of law is to:
   A) protect the rich
   B) ensure equality before the law
   C) punish offenders arbitrarily
   D) promote the interest of the ruling class
**Answer: B**

**20.** Which of the following is a function of the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) in Nigeria?
   A) Appointing judges
   B) Conducting population census
   C) Registering political parties
   D) Controlling the armed forces
**Answer: C**

**21.** Which of the following is an effect of drug abuse on the society?
   A) Improved academic performance
   B) Increased youth empowerment
   C) Increase in crime rate
   D) Better family relationships
**Answer: C**

**22.** A condition which leads to the spread of HIV/AIDS is:
   A) sharing of sterilized needles
   B) unprotected sexual intercourse
   C) dining with people living with the virus
   D) using screened blood for transfusion
**Answer: B**

**23.** Which of the following promotes responsible parenthood?
   A) Large family size
   B) Child neglect
   C) Financial stability
   D) Inconsistent discipline
**Answer: C**

**24.** The political right of a citizen includes the right to:
   A) freedom of movement
   B) freedom of speech
   C) be voted for
   D) sue and be sued
**Answer: C**

**25.** The right to freedom of expression can be limited by:
   A) the legislature
   B) the police
   C) slander
   D) security agents
**Answer: C**

**26.** The independence of the judiciary can be guaranteed by:
   A) strong executive
   B) security of tenure for judges
   C) strong armed forces
   D) strong intergovernmental relation
**Answer: B**

**27.** For a country to experience political stability, its citizens must be:
   A) politically active and morally responsible
   B) relaxed in their responsibilities
   C) supported by leaders in government
   D) indifferent to government policies
**Answer: A**

**28.** A person who encourages others to disobey constituted authority is guilty of:
   A) civil disobedience
   B) hooliganism
   C) insubordination
   D) anarchy
**Answer: C**

**29.** One of the ways of preventing the spread of HIV/AIDS is through:
   A) use of unsterilized sharp objects
   B) abstinence and faithfulness
   C) sharing of personal effects
   D) traditional medicine only
**Answer: B**

**30.** One of the functions of local government is:
   A) settlement of intra party crisis
   B) making of bye laws
   C) formulation of monetary policies
   D) delimitation of constituencies
**Answer: B**

**31.** High crime rate in some communities in Nigeria is primarily due to:
   A) unemployment
   B) over-population
   C) lack of traffic signs
   D) absence of traditional rulers
**Answer: A**

**32.** A body established and entrusted with power and authority to direct the affairs of the state is called:
   A) political party
   B) interest group
   C) constituted authority
   D) civil society
**Answer: C**

**33.** One significance of democracy in Nigeria is that it promotes:
   A) secularism
   B) sectionalism
   C) regionalism
   D) constitutionalism
**Answer: D**

**34.** Standards, rules and criteria that determine acceptable norms in the society are referred to as:
   A) values
   B) attitudes
   C) conventions
   D) regulations
**Answer: A**

**35.** One of the political responsibilities of a citizen is:
   A) Exercising the right of franchise
   B) Participating in social gatherings
   C) Engaging in cultural debates
   D) Joining town hall meetings
**Answer: A**

**36.** Responsible parenting has the tendency of:
   A) encouraging egoistic tendencies
   B) preserving culture
   C) promoting positive values
   D) promoting peaceful coexistence
**Answer: C**

**37.** Which of the following tasks is not related to civic duties?
   A) participation in environmental sanitation
   B) payment of taxes and rates
   C) obeying traffic rules
   D) having the right to self-control
**Answer: D**

**38.** Which of the following may be a reason for denying citizenship by naturalization in Nigeria?
   A) being of good character
   B) being over 21 years
   C) having lived in Nigeria for 10 years
   D) compromise of Nigerian security
**Answer: D**

**39.** The responsibilities of citizens to the state are synonymous with their:
   A) values
   B) standards
   C) privileges
   D) duties
**Answer: D**

**40.** Which of the following is not a function of the legislature?
   A) conferring titles
   B) declaring winners in an election
   C) making laws
   D) confirming appointments
**Answer: B**

**41.** Traffic regulations are mainly meant for:
   A) pedestrians only
   B) truck drivers only
   C) private house owners
   D) all road users
**Answer: D**

**42.** When opposition communities come together to discuss a way out of a pending disagreement, such a gathering is known as:
   A) political campaign
   B) economic partnership
   C) social gathering
   D) reconciliation
**Answer: D**

**43.** Which of the following is a social right of a citizen?
   A) right to life
   B) right to education
   C) right to vote
   |D) right to fair hearing
**Answer: B**

**44.** The characteristic of an individual who is not contented is:
   A) greediness
   B) laziness
   C) humility
   D) politeness
**Answer: A**

**45.** Citizenship can be acquired through all the following except:
   A) Birth
   B) Registration
   C) Force
   D) Naturalization
**Answer: C**

**46.** Cultism is characterizing by all the following EXCEPT:
   A) violent disposition
   B) secret initiation
   C) community development
   |D) use of dangerous weapons
**Answer: C**

**47.** A person who takes drugs without prescription is involved in:
   A) Drug addiction
   B) Drug trafficking
   C) Drug abuse
   D) Drug distribution
**Answer: C**

**48.** One aim of civic education in schools is to:
   A) encourage students to join cults
   B) help students identify their rights and duties
   C) promote examination malpractice
   D) teach students how to evade taxes
**Answer: B**

**49.** Traffic signs are designed to:
   A) ensure safety on the road
   B) instruct drivers on the use of sirens only
   C) encourage competition among road users
   D) promote reckless driving
**Answer: A**

**50.** The basic unit of the society is the:
   A) School
   B) Church/Mosque
   C) Family
   D) Community
**Answer: C**
