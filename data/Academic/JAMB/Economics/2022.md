# JAMB Economics Past Questions - 2022

## Questions

**1.** What is the primary focus of microeconomics?
   N) National income levels
   C) Consumer and firm behavior
   M) Monetary policy effects
   G) Government intervention in the economy
   **Answer: B**

**2.** In a market economy, prices are primarily determined by:
   G) Government regulations
   S) Supply and demand
   P) Production costs
   C) Consumer preferences
   **Answer: B**

**3.** What concept explains the situation where an increase in income leads to a decrease in the quantity demanded of a good?
   I) Inferior goods
   S) Substitute goods
   C) Complementary goods
   N) Normal goods
   **Answer: A**

**4.** Which of the following is NOT considered a component of Gross Domestic Product (GDP)?
   C) Consumption
   I) Investment
   G) Government spending
   E) Exports minus imports
   **Answer: D**

**5.** The law of diminishing marginal utility states that:
   T) Total utility increases with quantity consumed
   M) Marginal utility eventually declines as consumption increases
   C) Consumption level determines the price of goods
   U) Utility is the same for all consumers
   **Answer: B**

**6.** What does the term 'opportunity cost' refer to?
   T) The total cost of a decision made
   **Answer: B**

**7.** In the context of international trade, what does the term 'comparative advantage' mean?
   T) The advantage of having more natural resources
   E) Economic benefits derived from trade tariffs
   **Answer: B**

**8.** Which of the following is a characteristic of monopolistic competition?
   F) Few firms, high barriers to entry
   M) Many firms, differentiated products
   P) Price takers
   S) Single seller
   **Answer: B**

**9.** What is the primary tool used by central banks to control inflation?
   C) Controlling wage levels
   A) Adjusting the money supply
   R) Regulating foreign exchange rates
   **Answer: B**

**10.** The term 'capital flight' refers to:
   T) The movement of capital within a nation
   I) Investors withdrawing assets from a country
   G) Government subsidies for local businesses
   **Answer: C**

**11.** What is a key drawback of using GDP as a measure of economic well-being?
   I) It considers leisure time
   **Answer: A**

**12.** What type of unemployment occurs when there is a mismatch between workers' skills and the skills needed for available jobs?
   C) Cyclical unemployment
   F) Frictional unemployment
   S) Seasonal unemployment
   **Answer: C**

**13.** The Laffer Curve illustrates the relationship between:
   T) Tax rates and tax revenue
   S) Supply and demand
   I) Inflation and unemployment
   M) Monetary policy and interest rates
   **Answer: A**

**14.** Which of the following indicates a recession?
   I) Increase in the stock market
   D) Decline in real GDP for two consecutive quarters
   H) High inflation over a short period
   **Answer: B**
