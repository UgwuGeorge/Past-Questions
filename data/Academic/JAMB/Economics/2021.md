# JAMB Economics Past Questions - 2021

## Questions

**1.** What is the primary objective of monetary policy?
   T) To improve trade balance
   **Answer: A**

**2.** Which of the following is a characteristic of a monopoly market structure?
   M) Many sellers
   S) Single seller
   P) Perfect information
   L) Low barriers to entry
   **Answer: B**

**3.** The law of demand states that:
   P) Price and quantity demanded are inversely related
   Q) Quantity demanded remains constant regardless of price
   S) Supply increases with an increase in price
   **Answer: B**

**4.** Which economic concept measures the responsiveness of quantity demanded to a change in price?
   I) Income elasticity
   C) Cross-price elasticity
   P) Price elasticity of demand
   S) Supply elasticity
   **Answer: C**

**5.** What is the term for costs that do not change regardless of the level of production?
   V) Variable costs
   M) Marginal costs
   F) Fixed costs
   T) Total costs
   **Answer: C**

**6.** The concept of opportunity cost refers to:
   T) The total benefit received from a decision
   **Answer: B**

**7.** Which of the following is NOT a function of the Central Bank?
   I) Issuing currency
   R) Regulating commercial banks
   C) Conducting fiscal policy
   M) Managing foreign reserves
   **Answer: C**

**8.** In the long run, what happens to profits in a perfectly competitive market?
   T) They fluctuate wildly
   **Answer: C**

**9.** The marginal utility theory suggests that:
   U) Utility is quantifiable in monetary terms
   A) Additional units yield decreasing satisfaction
   M) More consumption always increases total utility
   **Answer: B**

**10.** Inflation measures:
   T) The level of employment in an economy
   **Answer: B**

**11.** What does GDP measure?
   T) Total income earned by residents
   **Answer: C**

**12.** Which of the following is an example of a public good?
   A) A restaurant meal
   N) National defense
   P) Private education
   **Answer: B**

**13.** What is the primary function of supply-side economic policies?
   T) To control inflation rates
   **Answer: B**

**14.** In the context of international trade, the term 'comparative advantage' refers to:
   T) The overall economic strength of a country
   **Answer: B**
