# JAMB Economics Past Questions - 2023

## Questions

**1.** What is the primary function of money in an economy?
   S) Standard of deferred payment
   M) Medium of exchange
   U) Unit of account
   **Answer: B**

**2.** In a perfectly competitive market, which of the following characteristics does NOT apply?
   H) Homogeneous products
   M) Many buyers and sellers
   P) Perfect information
   **Answer: C**

**3.** Which economic system relies primarily on private ownership and the free market?
   S) Socialism
   C) Capitalism
   F) Feudalism
   **Answer: C**

**4.** What does the term 'opportunity cost' refer to?
   T) The benefit of the best alternative chosen
   **Answer: B**

**5.** What is the effect of an increase in demand on equilibrium price and quantity?
   D) Decrease price, increase quantity
   I) Increase price, increase quantity
   N) No change in price or quantity
   **Answer: C**

**6.** Which of the following is NOT a source of government revenue?
   T) Taxes
   B) Borrowing
   F) Foreign aid
   D) Dividends from state enterprises
   **Answer: C**

**7.** What is meant by 'inflation targeting'?
   S) Setting a specific exchange rate
   C) Controlling inflation by changing interest rates
   G) Government regulation of prices
   R) Reducing taxes to stimulate growth
   **Answer: B**

**8.** Which of the following best describes 'market failure'?
   S) Surplus in the market
   I) Inefficient allocation of resources
   H) High level of competition
   E) Equilibrium of supply and demand
   **Answer: B**

**9.** The principal-agent problem occurs when...
   O) Owners and managers have aligned interests
   T) The agent's actions do not reflect the principal's interests
   M) Market forces dictate prices
   C) Competition drives down prices
   **Answer: B**

**10.** What impact does an increase in the price of a substitute good generally have?
   D) Decrease in demand for the original good
   I) Increase in supply of the original good
   N) No effect on demand
   **Answer: B**

**11.** What is the marginal propensity to consume (MPC)?
   T) The tax liability of an individual
   **Answer: A**

**12.** In the context of fiscal policy, what does 'crowding out' refer to?
   I) Increased public spending on infrastructure
   D) Decrease in consumer spending
   R) Reduction in private sector investment
   **Answer: A**

**13.** Which of the following is a characteristic of a monopoly?
   M) Many sellers
   U) Unique products
   F) Free entry and exit
   P) Perfect information
   **Answer: B**

**14.** What does a Lorenz curve illustrate?
   T) The relationship between income and consumption
   I) Inflation rates over time
   G) Government spending versus tax revenue
   **Answer: B**

**15.** Which of the following best defines GDP at purchasing power parity (PPP)?
   T) The total value of all goods and services produced
   A) Adjustment of GDP to reflect cost of living differences
   G) GDP calculated at market exchange rates
   C) Comparison of economic output between nations
   **Answer: B**
