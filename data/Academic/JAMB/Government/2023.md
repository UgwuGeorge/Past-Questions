# JAMB Government Past Questions - 2023

## Questions

**1.** Which of the following concepts best describes the division of government into three branches?
   C) Checks and Balances
   F) Federalism
   S) Separation of Powers
   J) Judicial Review
   **Answer: C**

**2.** The term 'sovereignty' refers to what aspect of a state?
   T) The approval of international treaties
   **Answer: B**

**3.** Which document is considered the supreme law of a nation?
   T) The Declaration of Independence
   J) Judicial verdicts
   **Answer: B**

**4.** Which principle allows the judiciary to review and invalidate laws that are in conflict with the constitution?
   S) Separation of Powers
   J) Judicial Review
   F) Federalism
   P) Popular Sovereignty
   **Answer: B**

**5.** What are the two main types of democracy?
   D) Direct and Representative
   M) Majoritarian and Consensus
   F) Federal and Unitary
   L) Liberal and Social
   **Answer: A**

**6.** The concept of 'rule of law' implies that:
   L) Laws may be ignored by the powerful
   E) Everyone is subject to the law equally
   J) Judges can make laws
   G) Government has no constraints
   **Answer: B**

**7.** The primary function of the executive branch of government is to:
   M) Make laws
   I) Interpret laws
   E) Enforce laws
   R) Revise laws
   **Answer: C**

**8.** Which of the following best describes a 'federal' system of government?
   C) Central authority holds absolute power
   P) Power is shared between national and regional governments
   U) Unitary system with decentralization
   T) Totalitarian regime
   **Answer: B**

**9.** The term 'civil liberties' refers to:
   R) Responsibilities of citizens
   P) Personal freedoms protected from government interference
   E) Economic rights
   **Answer: B**

**10.** Which amendment to the Constitution granted women the right to vote?
   1) 19th Amendment
   2) 26th Amendment
   **Answer: B**

**11.** The separation of the church and state is primarily intended to:
   P) Prevent governmental interference in religious affairs
   E) Establish a state religion
   I) Impose religious laws
   **Answer: B**

**12.** Which political ideology advocates for government control of the economy and redistribution of wealth?
   C) Conservatism
   L) Liberalism
   S) Socialism
   **Answer: C**

**13.** What document formally ended the American Revolutionary War?
   T) The Declaration of Independence
   **Answer: B**

**14.** The process by which voters can propose legislation is known as:
   R) Recall
   I) Initiative
   P) Plebiscite
   **Answer: C**

**15.** Which system is characterized by a single party controlling the government?
   P) Pluralism
   M) Multi-party System
   S) Single-party System
   B) Bipartisan System
   **Answer: C**
