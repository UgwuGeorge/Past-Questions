# JAMB Government Past Questions - 2020

## Questions

**1.** What is the primary function of the legislative arm of government?
   T) To enforce laws
   **Answer: B**

**2.** In a federal system of government, sovereignty is divided between the central government and what other component?
   T) The citizens
   **Answer: B**

**3.** Who is responsible for interpreting the laws in a government?
   T) The political parties
   **Answer: C**

**4.** Which model of governance is characterized by a concentration of power in a single authority?
   D) Democracy
   O) Oligarchy
   A) Anarchy
   **Answer: C**

**5.** What term describes the system where citizens vote directly on laws and policies?
   I) Indirect democracy
   D) Direct democracy
   R) Representative democracy
   T) Technocracy
   **Answer: B**

**6.** Which of the following is NOT a principle of rule of law?
   E) Equality before the law
   L) Legal certainty
   A) Arbitrary power
   T) Transparency in governance
   **Answer: C**

**7.** Which historical document limited the powers of the monarch in England?
   T) The English Constitution
   **Answer: A**

**8.** What type of government is ruled by a small group of elites?
   D) Democracy
   O) Oligarchy
   T) Theocracy
   M) Monarchy
   **Answer: B**

**9.** Which constitutional principle protects minority groups from the majority's rule?
   M) Minority rights
   C) Checks and balances
   S) Separation of powers
   **Answer: D**

**10.** In which government system do the executive and legislative branches operate separately and independently?
   P) Parliamentary system
   U) Unitary system
   C) Confederal system
   **Answer: A**

**11.** What is the main purpose of political parties in a democracy?
   T) To impose laws
   **Answer: B**

**12.** What is it called when a government imposes restrictions on freedom of speech?
   C) Censorship
   S) Subversion
   P) Propaganda
   **Answer: A**

**13.** Which of the following best describes the social contract theory?
   G) Government exists solely for military purposes
   C) Citizens have no obligation to government
   **Answer: B**

**14.** Which document serves as the supreme law of the land in the United States?
   T) The Federalist Papers
   **Answer: B**

**15.** What principle requires branches of government to oversee one another to prevent abuse of power?
   S) Separation of powers
   C) Checks and balances
   F) Federalism
   P) Popular sovereignty
   **Answer: B**
