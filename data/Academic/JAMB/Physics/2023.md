**1.** A simple pendulum, has a period of 5.77 seconds. When the pendulum is shortened by 3 m, the period is 4.60 seconds. Calculate the new length of the pendulum
   A)  5.23 m
   B) 6.42 m
   C) 4.87 m
   D) 7.26 m
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Given information:

Initial period, T₁ = 5.77 s
Final period, T₂ = 4.60 s
Change in length = 3 m (shortened)
Initial length, L₁ = ?
Final length, L₂ = L₁ - 3
g = 10 m/s² (or 9.8 m/s²)

Solution:
Step 1: Write period formula for both cases
For a simple pendulum:
T = 2π√(L/g)
Initial pendulum:
T₁ = 2π√(L₁/g)  -----(1)
Shortened pendulum:
T₂ = 2π√(L₂/g) = 2π√[(L₁-3)/g]  -----(2)
Step 2: Form ratio to eliminate constants
Dividing equation (1) by equation (2):
T₁/T₂ = √(L₁/(L₁-3))
5.77/4.60 = √(L₁/(L₁-3))
1.254 = √(L₁/(L₁-3))
Step 3: Square both sides
(1.254)² = L₁/(L₁-3)
1.573 = L₁/(L₁-3)
Step 4: Solve for L₁
1.573(L₁-3) = L₁
1.573L₁ - 4.719 = L₁
1.573L₁ - L₁ = 4.719
0.573L₁ = 4.719
L₁ = 8.23 m
Step 5: Calculate new length
L₂ = L₁ - 3
L₂ = 8.23 - 3
L₂ = 5.23 m*

**8.** A wire of radius 0.2 mm is extended by 0.5% of its length when supported by a load of 1.5 kg. Determine the Young's modulus for the material of the wire. [Take g = 10 ms⁻²]
   A) 2.4×10¹⁰ (Nm⁻²)
   B) 1.5×10¹⁰ (Nm⁻²)
   C)  2.4×10⁹ (Nm⁻²)
   D)  1.3×10¹⁰ (Nm⁻²)
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Given information:

Radius, r = 0.2 mm = 0.2 × 10⁻³ m = 2 × 10⁻⁴ m
Extension = 0.5% of original length
Strain, ε = 0.5/100 = 0.005
Mass, m = 1.5 kg
Acceleration due to gravity, g = 10 m/s²
Young's modulus, E = ?

Solution:
Using Young's modulus formula:
E = Stress/Strain = (F/A)/ε
Step 1: Calculate the force (weight)
F = mg
F = 1.5 × 10
F = 15 N
Step 2: Calculate cross-sectional area
A = πr²
A = π × (2 × 10⁻⁴)²
A = π × 4 × 10⁻⁸
A = 3.142 × 4 × 10⁻⁸
A = 1.257 × 10⁻⁷ m²
Step 3: Calculate stress
Stress = F/A
Stress = 15/(1.257 × 10⁻⁷)
Stress = 1.193 × 10⁸ N/m²
Step 4: Calculate Young's modulus
E = Stress/Strain
E = (1.193 × 10⁸)/0.005
E = 2.386 × 10¹⁰ N/m²
E ≈ 2.4 × 10¹⁰ N/m²*

**9.** The near point of a patient's eye is 50.0 cm. What power (in diopters) must a corrective lens have to enable the eye to see clearly an object 25.0 cm away?
   A) 2 diopters
   B) 2.5 diopters
   C) 0.5 diopters
   D) 3 diopters
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Given information:

Near point (without correction) = 50.0 cm
Desired object distance, u = 25.0 cm
Image distance (where eye can see clearly), v = -50.0 cm (virtual image)
Power, P = ?

Solution:
Step 1: Understand the requirement
The lens must form a virtual image at 50 cm (the eye's near point) when the object is at 25 cm. This allows the eye to see the object clearly.
Step 2: Apply lens formula
1/f = 1/v - 1/u
Sign convention:

Object distance, u = -25.0 cm (object is on the left)
Image distance, v = -50.0 cm (virtual image on the same side)

Step 3: Calculate focal length
1/f = 1/(-50) - 1/(-25)
1/f = -1/50 + 1/25
1/f = -1/50 + 2/50
1/f = 1/50
f = 50 cm = 0.50 m
Step 4: Calculate power
Power, P = 1/f (in meters)
P = 1/0.50
P = 2 diopters*
