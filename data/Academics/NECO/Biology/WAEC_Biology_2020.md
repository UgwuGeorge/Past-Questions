# NECO Biology Past Questions (2020)

## Objectives

**1.** The energy currency of the cell is
   A) DNA
   B) ATP
   C) RNA
   D) Glucose
   **Answer: B**
   *Explanation: ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) is the primary energy currency used by cells.*

**2.** Which of the following is a decomposer?
   A) Mushroom
   B) Moss
   C) Fern
   D) Algae
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Mushrooms are fungi that decompose dead organic matter and recycle nutrients.*

**3.** The part of the eye that controls the amount of light entering is the
   A) Retina
   B) Cornea
   C) Iris
   D) Lens
   **Answer: C**
   *Explanation: The iris contains muscles that adjust pupil size to control light entry.*

**4.** In plants, the male reproductive part is the
   A) Ovary
   B) Stigma
   C) Anther
   D) Petal
   **Answer: C**
   *Explanation: The anther produces pollen grains containing male gametes.*

**5.** Which of the following is a deficiency disease caused by lack of vitamin C?
   A) Rickets
   B) Scurvy
   C) Beriberi
   D) Pellagra
   **Answer: B**
   *Explanation: Scurvy is caused by deficiency of vitamin C and leads to bleeding gums and poor wound healing.*

**6.** The smallest blood vessels in the body are the
   A) Arteries
   B) Veins
   C) Capillaries
   D) Arterioles
   **Answer: C**
   *Explanation: Capillaries have thin walls allowing exchange of substances between blood and tissues.*

**7.** Which organelle contains digestive enzymes?
   A) Mitochondria
   B) Ribosome
   C) Lysosome
   D) Chloroplast
   **Answer: C**
   *Explanation: Lysosomes contain hydrolytic enzymes for breaking down waste materials and cellular debris.*

**8.** The process of conversion of glucose to pyruvate is called
   A) Glycolysis
   B) Krebs cycle
   C) Electron transport
   D) Photosynthesis
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Glycolysis is the breakdown of glucose to pyruvate, occurring in the cytoplasm.*

**9.** In humans, the sex chromosomes are
   A) XX and XY
   B) X and Y
   C) X and X
   D) Y and Y
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Females are XX and males are XY in humans.*

**10.** Which part of the brain controls breathing and heart rate?
   A) Cerebrum
   B) Cerebellum
   C) Medulla oblongata
   D) Hypothalamus
   **Answer: C**
   *Explanation: The medulla oblongata controls vital autonomic functions including breathing and heart rate.*
