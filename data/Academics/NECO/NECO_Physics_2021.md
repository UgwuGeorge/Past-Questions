**1.** Calculate the temperature of 6 moles of an ideal gas at a pressure of 7.6 × 10⁶ Nm⁻² with a volume of 10⁻³ m³. [R = 8.3 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹]
   A) 201°C
   B) 126°C
   C) 153°C
   D) 185°C
   **Answer: C**
   *Explanation: Given information:

Number of moles (n) = 6 moles
Pressure (P) = 7.6 × 10⁶ Nm⁻²
Volume (V) = 10⁻³ m³
Gas constant (R) = 8.3 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹

Using the Ideal Gas Equation:
PV = nRT
Rearranging to find T:
T = PV/(nR)
Substituting values:
T = (7.6 × 10⁶ × 10⁻³)/(6 × 8.3)
T = 7,600/49.8
T = 152.6°C ≈ 153°C*
