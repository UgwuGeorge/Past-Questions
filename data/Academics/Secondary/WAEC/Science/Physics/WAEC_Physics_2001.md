**1.** One of the following is a scalar quantity?
   A) weight
   B) momentum
   C) potential energy
   D) displacement
   **Answer: C**
   *Explanation: Potential energy is a scalar quantity as it only has magnitude and no directional component. Weight, momentum, and displacement are all vector quantities because they require both magnitude and direction for complete description.*

**5.** If a body moves with a constant speed and at the same time undergoes an acceleration, its motion is said to be
   A) oscillation
   B) circular
   C) rotational
   D) rectilinear
   **Answer: B**
   *Explanation: Circular motion is characterized by constant speed with continuous acceleration (centripetal acceleration) that changes the direction of velocity while maintaining constant magnitude.*
