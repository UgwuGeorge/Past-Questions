**2.** Jus Sanguinis is a principle that grants citizenship based on
   A) parental citizenship
   B) place of birth
   C) ethnic background
   D) length of residency
   **Answer: A**
   *Explanation: Jus Sanguinis is a principle of nationality law by which citizenship is determined or acquired by the nationality or ethnicity of one or both parents. This contrasts with jus soli, which is a principle by which citizenship is determined by place of birth.*

   *Explanation: In a federal system, the exclusive list designates responsibilities solely under the federal government’s authority. Key functions like currency, armed forces, and national defense are included in this list to ensure national unity, security, and uniformity.*
