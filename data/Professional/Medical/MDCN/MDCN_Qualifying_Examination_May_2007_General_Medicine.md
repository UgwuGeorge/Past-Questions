# MDCN Qualifying Examination May 2007 - General Medicine

## Questions

**1.** A 45-year-old male presents with sudden onset crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm. What is the most likely diagnosis?
   A) Pneumonia
   B) Myocardial Infarction
   C) Gastroesophageal Reflux
   D) Pulmonary Embolism
   **Answer: B**

**2.** Which of the following is the first-line treatment for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus in an obese patient?
   A) Insulin
   B) Glibenclamide
   C) Metformin
   D) Acarbose
   **Answer: C**

**3.** A 28-year-old G1P0 at 34 weeks presents with BP 160/110 mmHg and 3+ proteinuria. Diagnosis?
   A) Gestational Hypertension
   B) Preeclampsia
   C) Eclampsia
   D) Chronic Hypertension
   **Answer: B**

**4.** At what age should a child be able to sit without support?
   A) 4 months
   B) 6 months
   C) 9 months
   D) 12 months
   **Answer: B**
